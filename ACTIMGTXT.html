The Holy Grails of Sneakerheads

Jordan 1 Lost & Found

The Lost & Found Jordan 1s, part of Nike's reimagined series, tell a story of how sneakers were often lost in storage in the backrooms of sneaker shops before they were discovered again.

Jordan 4 Oxidized Green

The Air Jordan 4 “Manila” gets its name from the vibrant green color that dominates the upper portion of the shoe. This bold hue is a nod to the lush tropics of the Philippines, specifically Manila, which was once a major stopover for American GIs during World War II.

Jordan 4 Travis Scott Blue

The Air Jordan IV celebrates Travis Scott, hip-hop artist, record label founder and Houston sports fan extraordinaire. The collaborative design salutes to a bygone era of Houston football, when the team took the field in red, white and light blue.

Jordan 3 White Cement

The Air Jordan 3's original run only consisted of four colorways, three of which Jordan wore in an NBA setting. He favored the White Cement edition throughout the 1987-1988 regular season, and the colorway is most commonly associated with its appearance on his feet as he won the 1988 Slam Dunk Contest.
